In the afternoon after a brief lunch, I headed to the Javir shopping center to get my esim card for Ireland and took a lot of pictures on my way there about the street and architectures. I bought some postcards and a discounted green Claddagh ring on my way to the Whisky Museum.

Inside the museum, I learned a lot about how the whisky is been made and its history. During the old age, Irish people got introduced to alcohol by the perfume bring by the monks from the middle east. They later developed whisky to cure people from diseases and to drink during a party. The top of the whisky in the barrel is methane which is poisonous. There are a lot of illegal alcohol trade going on in Ireland compared in England and Scotland, and they got along with it by stating that they don't understand English. Because making alcohol is illegal at that time, people bury the barrel in the forest and sometimes forgot where they buried them :0 Although Ireland has a lot of potatoes, they use barley to make whisky. The flavor of whisky is from the combination of alcohol and the motion of shrinking and expanding of the wooden barrel. The sugar and flavor from the wood would then transport to the whisky through the temperature changes. If they mix the malt (processed the barley with hot water) with the bare barley, it will generate an oilier taste. Some brand of whisky is been purchased by French people when Irish whisky got unpopular.

I went to the EPIC Irish Emigration Museum and learned about the emigration history of Ireland. The museum is divided by several small sections with different topics, and I collected stamps on my museum passport for each section. In the history, the driving force of the Irish emigration is because of multiple reasons like political disagreement, starving, unrecognized identity. They emigrate not because of they want to travel, but because they have no choice. The unmarried women and gay population will be banished to the colony because the government think it's unworthy for them to live in Irish back at that time, and people leave their land due to starvation or financial crises. The traveling condition won't be easy and a lot of them died on their way to the new land.

This forced emigration, on the other hand, builds the history and fostered the culture communication and humanity development. The Irish emigrants contribute to multiple fields such as human right advocation, art, and sports industry. While they faced discrimination form the British, they thrived to make our world better.

Google as another big tech company other than Microsoft have similar style of the Office building. The interior design fully took advantage of vibrant colors which makes it feels modern and relaxing. Different than Microsoft which only have one main building, Google have a lot of small and packed buildings in the dockland of Ireland. Because of the large debt that the Ireland government faced in the history, the government sold a lot of buildings to foreign companies and Google acquired a lot of them that formed a google town.

Google feels more like an idea hub where employees could go anywhere inside the building they want to work. I think google really emphasized the value of ideas and did the best to make employees feel comfortable and relaxed in terms of working environment to help them be productive. Although the Google buildings that we went feels more packed than the Microsoft headquarters, they have prettier scenery from the window. They even have free food for employees! All googlers love this perk, but one of them told me that he tried not to eat the snacks form the company because most people gained weight when they first working for google. People from different part of the world come to Ireland to work for big tech companies. Where people from originally won't determine where they will go; the ability to adapt and fit into the culture with passion and knowledge is the key for the future.
